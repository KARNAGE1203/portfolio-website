# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers and technical recruiters evaluating Danish for full-time roles in UX/UI design and/or frontend development. They arrive with a job description in hand, spend 60–120 seconds scanning, and decide whether to read further or bounce. They are assessing craft, taste, and range — not just reading a CV. They may also include design directors doing a quick portfolio pass before an interview.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio that converts a hiring-manager first look into a conversation. It exists to demonstrate that Danish can both design and build — that the work shown is real, the thinking is clear, and the person behind it is someone worth meeting. Success = a recruiter forwarding the link internally or booking a call.

## Brand Personality

Curious, craft-driven, personal. The site should feel like it was made by a real human with a specific point of view — not like a template filled in. Visitors should leave thinking "this person clearly cares about what they make."

## Anti-references

- Dribbble-shot aesthetic: all polish, no substance — mockups floating in space with no context or thinking behind them
- Corporate/agency aesthetic: formal, suit-and-tie, enterprise-feeling, generic professionalism
- AI-generated slop: obvious scaffold — section eyebrows on every heading, gradient text, identical card grids, nothing that couldn't have been generated in 5 seconds

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** Danish is a UX/UI designer. The portfolio IS the portfolio piece. If it looks average, it undermines every case study.
2. **Show the thinking, not just the polish.** Hiring managers want to understand how Danish works, not just see finished screens. The work should tell a story.
3. **Personal without being precious.** Warmth and personality belong here, but never at the expense of clarity or craft.
4. **Restraint is a choice, not a default.** Every empty space should feel intentional. Nothing should be there because it was easy to add.
5. **One voice across every page.** Nav, hero, project pages, books page, contact — they should feel like they're all from the same person.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion support via `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)`. The audience is professional and likely on desktop, but mobile hiring (LinkedIn app, mobile browser) is a real path.
